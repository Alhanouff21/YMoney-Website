The Kuwaiti Dinar is the official currency of the state of Kuwait, issued by the Central Bank of Kuwait currently. The first Kuwaiti Dinar was introduced to the markets in 1960, replacing the Indian Rupee that was previously in use. Subsequently, the Central Bank of Kuwait issued six other currency releases, with the latest in 2014, announced on May 19, 2014. The Kuwaiti Dinar is divided into 1000 fils and is currently considered to have the highest exchange rate in the world. As of June 2008, the exchange rate of the Kuwaiti Dinar was approximately 3.78 US dollars and 2.4 euros. The first series of the twenty dinar denomination was released on February 9, 1986.
